iPad Apps That Use the Full Width of an External Display

The iPad provides support for external screens even without the new Stage Manager feature. Usually, it only provides mirroring which results in an odd pillar-boxed screen on a wide display. A few apps support external screens in their native resolution and wide aspect ratio. In this video I demonstrate the Apple Photos app, the Apple TV app, the video editing app LumaFusion, the mind mapping app MindNode, the muti-window browser app Shiftscreen, and the VNC app Edovia Screens with the iPad connected to a Raspberry Pi4.

Chaos Engine: Atari ST and STE

The game "The Chaos Engine" from the early 1990s by the Bitmap Brothers was released for the Commodore Amiga, the Sega MegaDrive and other systems. There was also a conversion for the Atari ST which famously had a palette of 512 colors, no hardware support for sprites and scrolling. On the Ataro STE the Chaos Engine took advantage of the extended palette of 4096 and its capability for hardware scrolling. This screen recording showcases the differences between the regular ST version and the extended STE version.
